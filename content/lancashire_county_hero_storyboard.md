## 2. Key finding

The current county voiceover script `fits` the existing `100 second` film structure.

Measured result:

- `16` voiced scenes
- all `16` voiced scenes fit inside their planned `5 second` windows
- no line rewrite is required before the first animatic
- the last `20 seconds` can stay music-led and image-led, which is the right emotional finish

That means the current county script is stable enough to move into:

- a scratch animatic
- a storyboard deck
- a first assembly edit

## 3. Storyboard rules

- Keep the first `20 seconds` visually grand but not empty.
- Show human use of place by `0:20`.
- Use the short lines in scenes `14`, `15` and `16` as `proof punctures`, not fast montage filler.
- Let the last `20 seconds` breathe with fewer cuts and broader music.
- Avoid on-screen copy until the end card.

## 5. Most important direction notes

### 5.1 First act

The opener works because it goes:

- `bay`
- `hill`
- `castle`
- `ballroom`

That sequence must stay intact unless a clearly better opening shot appears.

### 5.2 Middle act

The middle must feel:

- inhabited
- civic
- social
- grounded

If it becomes a scenic reel, the county film weakens immediately.

### 5.3 Proof act

The proof section at `1:05 to 1:20` is intentionally short.

That is correct.

Lancashire should feel emotionally convincing `before` it feels economically explained.

### 5.4 Final act

The end must not rush.

The silent final `20 seconds` are doing real work:

- emotional release
- public feeling
- invitation

Do not clutter them with facts or extra narration.
