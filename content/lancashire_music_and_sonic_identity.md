# Lancashire Music and Sonic Identity

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This note is about `music direction for Lancashire place-marketing`, not just a list of songs.

The main strategic question is:

- `What should Lancashire sound like if the county is being marketed as warm, proud, cinematic and economically alive?`

## 1. Executive recommendation

The best answer is:

- `commission or license an original theme that sounds like Lancashire`
- not `pick one existing famous song and build the whole county around it`

Why:

- one track rarely covers `coast + towns + hills + heritage + future`
- famous commercial songs are usually harder and more expensive to clear
- Lancashire’s strongest music cues come from `multiple traditions`, not one definitive anthem

The best sonic formula is:

- `human warmth`
- `northern character`
- `heritage texture`
- `modern lift`

In practice, that means:

- a strong melody
- real voices
- some sense of `ballroom / brass / choir / coast`
- modern drums, pulse or production so it does not feel museum-like

## 2. The strongest Lancashire music codes

## 2.1 Blackpool ballroom and Wurlitzer

This is one of the cleanest immediately recognisable Lancashire sounds.

Official tourism and attraction sources show:

- Blackpool Tower Ballroom is still marketed around live `Wurlitzer` organ music. Sources: [The Blackpool Tower Ballroom help page](https://www.theblackpooltower.com/plan-your-visit/help-centre/general-enquiries/can-i-have-afternoon-tea-in-the-tower-ballroom/), [The Wurlitzer Organists](https://www.theblackpooltower.com/wurtlizer-organists/).
- `Reginald Dixon` became synonymous with Blackpool, and his signature tune `Oh I Do Like to be Beside the Seaside` is explicitly described by Visit Lancashire as synonymous with Blackpool and its entertainment history. Source: [Visit Lancashire on Reginald Dixon](https://www.visitlancashire.com/blog/read/2021/08/vintage-blackpool-tram-to-be-renamed-in-honour-of-famous-blackpool-tower-organist-reginald-dixon-b902).

What this means for the campaign:

- the `Wurlitzer` is a brilliant `motif`
- it should usually be a `colour`, not the entire score

Best use:

- Blackpool cuts
- glamour edits
- heritage transitions
- opening or bridge moments

## 2.2 Lancashire’s choral and communal singing tradition

This is probably the most underused but strategically strongest county-wide sound.

Arts Lancashire’s `County of Song`, supported by Lancashire County Council, explicitly describes the county as having a `rich vocal tapestry`, `vibrant singing heritage`, and a desire to connect this tradition to new generations. Source: [County of Song](https://artslancashire.org.uk/county-of-song/).

The same programme highlights:

- the `Larks of Dean` in East Lancashire
- the role of community singing across every district
- current groups such as `Rossendale Male Voice Choir`, which won `Lancashire Choir of the Year` in `2022`

Sources: [County of Song](https://artslancashire.org.uk/county-of-song/), [Rossendale Male Voice Choir](https://rossendalemalevoicechoir.co.uk/about-us/).

What this means for the campaign:

- Lancashire should sound `sung`, not just programmed
- a choir, vocal ensemble, or layered communal vocal bed is one of the best ways to make civic pride feel real

Best use:

- hero film
- civic-pride edits
- endings
- family and community moments

## 2.3 Morecambe community rhythm and street energy

`More Music` in Morecambe is one of the county’s strongest live examples of music as place-making.

Official More Music material describes:

- a music and education charity in the West End of Morecambe with `30 years` of community music work
- projects like `Baybeat Streetband`, described as a multi-generational community streetband with `drums, percussion and horns`

Sources: [More Music home page](https://moremusic.org.uk/), [Beats of Morecambe Bay](https://moremusic.org.uk/news/beats-of-morecambe-bay/).

What this means for the campaign:

- Lancashire can sound `street-level`, `festive`, and `collective`
- percussion and horns can give the county energy without defaulting to generic trailer music

Best use:

- festival content
- town-centre content
- Morecambe and bayfront activations
- social cutdowns

## 2.4 Lancashire singer-songwriter warmth

The best emotional bridge between `heritage` and `modernity` is often the singer-songwriter lane.

The strongest current on-brief reference I found is `Little Boots` and her Lancashire commission.

More Music’s interview with her says:

- she made a Lancashire-inspired song called `red rose`
- it was inspired by growing up in `Lancashire`, especially `Blackpool`, the `Fylde Coast` and `Wyre`
- she says the song is about `returning home`, `nostalgic journey`, and `togetherness`

Source: [An Interview With Little Boots](https://moremusic.org.uk/news/an-interview-with-little-boots/).

That is extremely close to the emotional job Lancashire’s main marketing film needs to do.

Best use:

- the `Little Boots / red rose` approach is the best emotional reference point for a commissioned county theme

## 2.5 Prestigious Lancashire voice

If Lancashire wants a higher-prestige, broader-age soundtrack reference, `Alfie Boe` is strong.

Penguin Random House describes him as `Lancashire-born` and `from the fishing port of Fleetwood`. Source: [Alfie Boe biography](https://www.penguinrandomhouse.com/authors/2304256/alfie-boe/).

What this means for the campaign:

- operatic or neo-classical grandeur can work for `prestige`, `sea`, `legacy`, and `big-emotion` edits
- but full classical treatment should be used selectively so the campaign does not become too stately or older-skewing

Best use:

- ceremonial edits
- premium tourism
- bay / coast / heritage uplift

## 2.6 Blackpool / Fylde pop nostalgia and longing

There is also a useful `nostalgic North / coastal pop` lane around `Rae Morris`.

Evidence used here:

- Little Boots explicitly calls Rae Morris `another Blackpool songwriter` and picks Rae’s `Heading North for Christmas` as a track that sums up her own annual return to family. Source: [An Interview With Little Boots](https://moremusic.org.uk/news/an-interview-with-little-boots/).
- Rae Morris also has a track titled `the old blackpool tower`. Source: [Apple Music track page](https://music.apple.com/gb/song/1652564875).

What this means for the campaign:

- Lancashire can credibly use a `wistful, melodic, northern-homecoming` sound
- this is very good for softer coast, winter, nostalgia and homecoming edits

## 2.7 Burnley edge and younger modernity

If the campaign needs a younger, more contemporary and less heritage-heavy lane, `Cody Frost` is the clearest current music reference I found.

Apple Music identifies Cody Frost as being `from Burnley, Lancashire, England`, and describes `ANATOMY` as `alt-pop rippers from the rising Burnley artist`. Sources: [Cody Frost artist page](https://music.apple.com/sb/artist/cody-frost/1101345290), [ANATOMY album page](https://music.apple.com/gb/album/anatomy/1768301844).

What this means for the campaign:

- Lancashire has a real `alt`, `punk`, `hyperpop` and youth-facing edge available if needed
- but this should sit in youth, nightlife, sport or creator cutdowns rather than the universal county hero film

## 3. Best music direction by campaign use

## 3.1 Main hero film

Best route:

- `commission an original track`

Best sonic recipe:

- felt piano or warm keys
- subtle `Wurlitzer-style` opening colour
- rising strings or pads
- understated percussion building into a stronger pulse
- choir or layered human voices at the emotional peak
- optional brass or horn lift, but not full brass-band pastiche

Closest references:

- `Little Boots / red rose`
- `County of Song`
- `Blackpool Wurlitzer`

## 3.2 Coast and glamour edits

Best route:

- ballroom rhythm
- organ motif
- elegant drums
- strings

Closest references:

- `Reginald Dixon / Wurlitzer`
- Blackpool ballroom heritage
- `Rae Morris` for softer coastal longing

## 3.3 Town, market and community edits

Best route:

- percussion
- horns
- choir
- street energy

Closest references:

- `Baybeat Streetband`
- County of Song community singing
- Rossendale Male Voice Choir

## 3.4 Youth, nightlife and creator edits

Best route:

- modern alt-pop, synthpop, electronic or left-of-centre pop production

Closest references:

- `Cody Frost`
- `Little Boots`

## 3.5 Humour, food and identity-led socials

Best route:

- use novelty and dialect music only in sidecar content

Closest references:

- `The Lancashire Hotpots`

Important note:

- this is great for social, food, humour, local-identity and lighter campaign moments
- it is not the right sound for the core county hero film

Evidence used:

- Visit Lancashire event pages show `The Lancashire Hotpots` as a recognised live touring part of the wider Lancashire cultural ecosystem. Sources: [Visit Lancashire Hotpots listing](https://www.visitlancashire.com/whats-on/the-lancashire-hotpots-p1159500), [Marketing Lancashire Lancashire Day coverage mentioning The Lancashire Hotpots](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/news/this-is-lancashire-film-showcases-the-beauty-of-the-county/).

## 4. What to avoid

- `Generic corporate ukulele`
  Too safe, too weak, and not specific to Lancashire.

- `Overblown trailer braams`
  Makes the county feel fake-epic rather than emotionally true.

- `Novelty comedy as the main theme`
  Works for side content, weakens the county’s seriousness if used as the central score.

- `Pure heritage pastiche`
  Lancashire needs to feel current and forward-moving, not stuck in sepia.

- `One famous pop song that swallows the place`
  The county story should stay bigger than the track.

## 5. Licensing reality

If the film uses an existing commercial song:

- PRS says sync uses are case-by-case, fees vary by use and track popularity, and if you use an original recording you also need the recording-rights owner. Source: [PRS commercial sync licensing](https://www.prsformusic.com/licences/releasing-music-products/commercial-music-sync-licensing).

If the project wants easier and cheaper options:

- MCPS Production Music offers `one licence, one million tracks` and positions itself as a `simple, affordable music licensing` route for sync. Source: [MCPS Production Music](https://mcpsproductionmusic.co.uk/).

Practical conclusion:

- `best quality and most ownable`: commission an original Lancashire theme
- `fastest compliant fallback`: use production music and brief it around Lancashire cues

## 6. Best practical recommendation

If I had to choose one direction for the main Lancashire campaign now, it would be:

- `a newly commissioned theme`
- inspired by `Little Boots' red rose` emotional logic
- coloured with a `Blackpool Wurlitzer` motif
- lifted by `choir / communal voices`
- produced with modern drums and low-end so it feels current

That would let Lancashire sound:

- proud
- warm
- cinematic
- recognisably northern
- and not trapped in one era
