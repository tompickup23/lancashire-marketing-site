# Lancashire County Strategy

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This is the new `county-level` top sheet for the Lancashire marketing project.

Use it as the main strategic layer above:

- borough strategies
- division anchors
- footage research
- talent and reserve pools
- event and monetisation work

The job of this document is to answer four county-wide questions:

- `What is the Lancashire story?`
- `What content should carry it?`
- `What should Lancashire sound like?`
- `Who and what should be in the top-level county strategy?`

## 1. The county proposition

The strongest Lancashire proposition remains:

- public line: `Make our Lancashire yours`
- internal creative test: `Big character. Real warmth. Proper Lancashire.`

That should hold together:

- tourism
- civic pride
- inward investment
- cultural programming
- major events
- partner and stakeholder work

The county should feel:

- warm
- substantial
- culturally alive
- visually rich
- emotionally true
- modern without pretending not to have heritage

## 2. The county content strategy

The biggest strategic rule is simple:

- Lancashire must not be marketed as `just hills`
- and it must not be marketed as `just a pile of attractions`

The county content stack should combine:

- `coast and bay`
  Morecambe Bay, Lytham, St Annes, Fleetwood, Cleveleys, Blackpool.

- `landscape and weather`
  Pendle Hill, Bowland, moorland roads, ridge lines, big skies.

- `proud towns and city character`
  Burnley, Preston, Lancaster, Blackburn, Accrington, Chorley, Nelson, Clitheroe.

- `heritage and memory`
  Blackpool Tower, Towneley, Clitheroe Castle, Lancaster Castle, mills, markets, piers, dance and variety culture.

- `food and products`
  Lancashire tea, hotpot, cheeses, crisps, sauce, markets, pubs, chefs, farm shops, hampers.

- `future-facing economic proof`
  Eden Project Morecambe, South Ribble innovation corridor, BAE Warton / Samlesbury, Vital Energi, Silicon Sands, Pendle manufacturing, Lancaster University.

- `people in the frame`
  supporters, traders, chefs, dancers, walkers, makers, schoolchildren, families, festival crowds, creators, volunteers.

## 4. County music strategy

The best county-level music answer is:

- `build a recognisable Lancashire sound`
- not `pick one random famous song`

The strongest top-level music formula is:

- `Blackpool Wurlitzer / ballroom colour`
- `County of Song / choir / communal voice`
- `Little Boots / red rose` emotional logic
- `modern production` so it feels current

That means:

- the hero film should ideally use a `commissioned original theme`
- faster cutdowns can use production music briefed around Lancashire cues

Top-level county sound references:

- `Blackpool Wurlitzer`
- `County of Song`
- `More Music`
- `Little Boots / red rose`

Secondary references for cutdowns:

- `Rae Morris`
- `Alfie Boe`
- `Cody Frost`
- `The Lancashire Hotpots` for lighter side content only

## 8. County monetisation logic

The big county monetisation lesson from the current work is:

- Lancashire should monetise `around` the event and campaign platform
- not expect every headline number to become direct council income

This matters most for:

- `Tour de France 2027`
- `major festivals`
- `food and hospitality`
- `county-wide partner activity`

The right county commercial model is:

- visitor spend capture
- weekend packaging
- partner inventory
- supplier capture
- trade and investment programming
- first-party audience capture
- post-event legacy products

## 9. Geography logic

This new architecture should be used like this:

- `county strategy`
  Sets the master line, content stack, music, people and priority proof.

- `borough strategy`
  Localises the county story through borough council geography.

- `reserve pool`
  Preserves maybes, second-wave leads, discovery sources and lower-confidence material without polluting the top tier.

- `division layer`
  Used for planning, filming, partner outreach and local cutdowns where necessary.

## 10. What belongs in the county hero film

Must include:

- coast and bay scale
- one or two major hills / upland icons
- proud towns and civic streets
- food and product texture
- at least one future-facing economic proof point
- real people using place
- a small number of trusted Lancashire faces

Must not become:

- empty drone montage
- politician-led message film
- nostalgia-only archive reel
- celebrity wallpaper
- one-town promo pretending to be the county

## 11. Final county rule

If a piece of work cannot clearly say:

- `why Lancashire`
- `why this county now`
- `why this person or place earns its slot`

then it belongs in the reserve pool, not the top-level strategy.
