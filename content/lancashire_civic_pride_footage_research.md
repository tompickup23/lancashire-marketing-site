# Lancashire Civic Pride Footage Research

Checked on March 20, 2026.

This is a wider research pack for a Lancashire civic pride / county marketing film, with a tourism-first angle.

Working assumptions:
- The film should make Lancashire feel epic, proud, culturally rich, economically alive, and worth visiting.
- For county marketing, it makes sense to include Blackpool and Blackburn with Darwen as well as Lancashire County Council areas, because official visitor-economy and tourism marketing for Lancashire is broader than county-council geography.
- I have separated `free reusable footage/media sources` from `good research/reference sources that are not automatically free to reuse`.

## 1. What Lancashire is officially marketing right now

These are the strongest current signals from official Lancashire tourism and economic-development sources:

- Lancashire’s latest official visitor economy infographic says the county had `68.6 million tourism visits` in `2024`, generated `£5.7 billion`, and supported `57,068` full-time-equivalent jobs. Source: [Lancashire STEAM 2024 infographic][steam-2024].
- Marketing Lancashire says Lancashire’s visitor economy is recovering strongly and positions the county as good-value, accessible, and sustainable for both day trips and short breaks. Source: [Latest visitor economy survey][steam-2023-news].
- Marketing Lancashire is publicly pushing Lancashire’s coast, events, attractions, food, beaches, and heritage, and is explicitly framing tourism as something that supports wider communities and sectors. Source: [Lancashire Tourism Awards 2026][tourism-awards-2026].
- Visit Lancashire and Marketing Lancashire describe the county as a place of `contrasting landscapes`, `wide open spaces`, `diverse heritage`, and `fantastic food and drink`. Source: [Visit Lancashire][visit-lancashire], [Make Lancashire Yours][make-lancashire-yours].
- The same Lancashire promotion material also leans hard into `coast + hills + outdoor activities + culture + hospitality + food`. Source: [Make Lancashire Yours][make-lancashire-yours].
- Official Lancashire tourism guidance is actively promoting film/TV connections, outdoor art, walking, cycling, coast, culture, and award-winning attractions. Sources: [Visit Lancashire summer guide][summer-guide], [Panopticons][panopticons], [Long Distance Walks][long-distance-walks], [Taste Lancashire][taste-lancashire].

Practical implication:
- The film should not just be “nice drone shots of hills”.
- The strongest county-marketing cut is: `big landscapes + proud towns + iconic attractions + food + culture + events + industry/heritage + people actually using the place`.

## 2.6 Using YouTube or social media clips safely

If the requirement is `fully free` and `safe to reuse`, treat platform footage very carefully.

- `YouTube`  
  Only treat a YouTube clip as reusable if it is explicitly marked `Creative Commons Attribution` or if you have direct written permission from the uploader / rightsholder. YouTube Help says uploaders choose either `Standard YouTube license` or `Creative Commons Attribution`, and that CC uploads can be reused subject to `CC BY` attribution rules. YouTube’s Terms of Service also restrict downloading or using content unless the service permits it or you have prior written permission. Sources: [YouTube license types][youtube-license-types], [YouTube Terms of Service][youtube-terms].

- `Instagram`  
  Public visibility is not a reuse licence. Instagram’s terms give Meta a licence to host and use what the uploader posts, but that does not give third parties a blanket right to reuse the content in a new film. Instagram’s help also states that its terms do not allow posting content that violates others’ IP rights. Sources: [Instagram Terms of Use][instagram-terms], [Instagram intellectual property help][instagram-ip].

- `TikTok`  
  Same principle: public viewing does not equal free reuse. TikTok’s terms give users a limited personal, non-commercial licence to access the service, and the uploader is still responsible for third-party rights in the content they post. Source: [TikTok Terms of Service][tiktok-terms].

Operational rule for this project:

- `Safe to treat as source footage`  
  Pexels, Pixabay, Wikimedia Commons, public-domain material, explicitly CC-licensed YouTube uploads, or creator footage with written permission.

- `Reference only unless separately cleared`  
  Normal YouTube uploads, Instagram Reels, TikToks, Facebook videos and reposted creator content.

- `Best practice`  
  Save a screenshot or PDF of the licence page on the day you download anything, because platform settings and creator choices can change later.

## 2.7 How to get better drone footage and social-star footage

The free-UHD search is only one route.

If Lancashire wants the campaign to look world-class, the acquisition plan should combine `free source footage`, `creator permissions`, and `new capture`.

### 2.7.1 Drone footage

Best routes:

- `Free stock and Commons first`  
  Keep a live pool of reusable aerials from [Pexels drone search][pexels-drone-search], [Pexels aerial search][pexels-aerial-search], [Pexels FPV search][pexels-fpv-search], and [Pixabay drone search][pixabay-drone-search]. This is the fastest route for broad atmosphere, transitions and generic county-scale movement.

- `Self-shoot the exact missing Lancashire icons`  
  The best use of new drone days is not generic coast or countryside. It is the current gap list: `Pendle Hill`, `Singing Ringing Tree`, `Towneley Hall`, `Samlesbury Hall`, `Fleetwood harbour`, `Lancaster Castle`, and premium `Ribble Valley` approaches. That is where exact civic-pride value is highest.

- `Use a local contributor callout`  
  Ask Lancashire drone photographers and filmmakers to submit existing 4K/UHD aerials under a written campaign licence or donation agreement. For a civic-pride campaign, this is often the cheapest path to exact local footage because many pilots already have the landmarks shot.

- `Capture both hero and utility passes`  
  Do not only brief cinematic orbits. Also capture simple reveals, straight push-ins, top-down textures, road follows, coastlines, market-town establishing shots and 5 to 10 second clean plates that editors can actually use repeatedly.

- `Shoot sunrise and blue-hour windows`  
  For Lancashire, the difference between average and powerful aerials is often weather and light rather than camera body.

Operational and compliance note:

- In the UK, drone flying is governed by the Civil Aviation Authority. The CAA’s Drone Code says you must keep your drone in sight, follow distance and safety rules, and check where you can fly. The CAA also requires registration in many cases. If people can be identified in your footage, the Information Commissioner’s Office says data protection law can apply depending on how you are using the footage. Sources: [CAA drones overview][caa-drones], [CAA Drone Code][caa-drone-code], [ICO drones guidance][ico-drones].

### 2.7.2 Better ways to get footage of social media stars

The right model is `licensed creator collaboration`, not ripping posts.

Best routes:

- `Get the creator to license existing footage directly`  
  Ask for the original master files, not a platform rip. This preserves quality and avoids platform-compression problems. In the agreement, cover where the footage can be used, how long for, whether paid ads are allowed, and whether the creator is also granting rights in any music, logos or third-party appearances.

- `Ask for new self-shot material`  
  Many social creators can supply much better county-specific footage by self-shooting on current phones or mirrorless cameras than anything available free online. Brief them for `4K`, clean music-free originals, horizontal and vertical versions, and a simple local story such as favourite place, favourite view, market, walk, beach, or family routine.

- `Run ambassador capture days`  
  Bring several Lancashire-rooted creators or public figures into one filmed county day rather than chasing separate permissions on random old clips. This is the fastest way to get exact hero footage that feels current.

- `Use platform-authorised creator tools when boosting content`  
  TikTok explicitly supports using content from `authorized creator accounts` through Spark Ads and linked business accounts. Source: [TikTok F.I.R.S.T. / Spark Ads guidance][tiktok-first]. For YouTube, creator-partnership tooling and open-call workflows are designed to connect brands with creators for authorised campaigns rather than unlicensed reuse. Source: [YouTube creator partnerships overview][youtube-creator-partnerships]. These routes are not usually free, but they are the cleanest scalable route for exact star or influencer content.

- `Use official creator marketplaces for outbound`  
  Meta has expanded creator marketplace workflows for brands, and TikTok has creator matching and creative services routes. Sources: [Meta creator marketplace update][meta-creator-marketplace], [TikTok One][tiktok-one], [TikTok Creative Exchange][tiktok-creative-exchange]. For this Lancashire project, those systems matter less as ad-buying products and more as a model: use a structured creator database, explicit permissions, and agreed deliverables.

- `Use stills and press assets for exact identity, motion for atmosphere`  
  If a named Lancashire person matters politically or culturally but no reusable UHD motion exists, combine a licensed still or Commons image with fresh county motion. That is often better than using poor-quality old social clips.

Minimum rights checklist for any creator-supplied footage:

- `Master files`
  Request the original exported files, not downloaded posts.

- `Resolution`
  Ask for `4K/UHD` plus both `16:9` and `9:16` if possible.

- `Music`
  Require no copyrighted music unless separately licensed.

- `Usage rights`
  Cover organic social, website, events, presentations and paid media if needed.

- `People and places`
  Confirm the creator has permission for anyone prominently featured and for any private-property locations where required.

- `Attribution`
  Decide in advance whether the campaign will credit the creator on-screen, in captions, or only in metadata / records.

## 3. What to include in the film

## 3.1 The highest-priority visual pillars

If the film is supposed to make Lancashire feel amazing, these are the strongest pillars.

- `The epic county reveal`  
  Use Morecambe Bay, Pendle Hill, Forest of Bowland, the Fylde coast, Blackpool skyline, and moorland roads. This gives scale immediately.

- `The county of landmarks`  
  Use Pendle Hill, the Singing Ringing Tree, Clitheroe Castle, Lancaster Castle, Lytham Windmill, Blackpool Tower, Samlesbury Hall, Hoghton Tower, Towneley Hall, Darwen Tower.

- `The county of coast and horizon`  
  Use Morecambe promenade, Stone Jetty, Heysham cliffs, Fleetwood seafront, Cleveleys promenade, Lytham Green, St Annes beach, Fairhaven Lake. Lancashire’s coast is one of its biggest tourism assets and official marketing keeps leaning into it.

- `The county that made things`  
  Use Queen Street Mill, Burnley canal/mill textures, Blackburn/Accrington industrial heritage, railway imagery, markets, docks, tram/frontage infrastructure, historic mills and workshops. Civic pride hits harder when the county looks productive, not just pretty.

- `The county of art, culture and identity`  
  Use Panopticons, Whitaker, Haworth Art Gallery, Harris, festivals, live performance spaces, public art, The TERN Project, Baylight, Tower Ballroom, Winter Gardens.

- `The county of food and welcome`  
  Official tourism branding keeps stressing food and drink. Use farm shops, distilleries, markets, bakeries, restaurants, cheese, ice cream, breweries, cafes, outdoor dining, and human hospitality.

- `The county of movement and fresh air`  
  Use walkers, cyclists, runners, paddleboarding, coastal walks, the Lancashire Coastal Way, Burnley Way, Wyre Way, Ribble Coast and Wetlands routes. Lancashire should look lived in and used, not empty.

## 6. Practical editing advice

If you want the film to feel genuinely proud rather than generic:

- Open with `scale`, not with a town-centre establishing shot.
- Cut between `wild landscape` and `urban/cultural confidence` so Lancashire looks varied and substantial.
- Do not let the film become only “witches, mills and old stone”. Balance heritage with seafront glamour, food, families, art, events, and current life.
- Include `people` wherever possible. Empty drone shots alone feel like property marketing, not civic pride.
- Use `local texture` that outsiders remember: trams, piers, brass, ballroom, fish markets, mills, moorland winds, birdlife, pies, pints, windmill, tower, promenade lights.

## 8. Global destination marketing best practice

Looking at current official destination marketing from places like New Zealand, Australia, California, Iceland, Britain and Scotland, the consistent pattern is not “show every attraction.” The pattern is:

- `One clear brand idea`  
  The best place brands are built around a memorable promise, not a directory.  
  New Zealand has kept `100% Pure New Zealand` alive for more than 25 years, and its latest campaign still puts that brand front and centre while refreshing the message around people, place and culture. Sources: [Tourism New Zealand campaign launch][tnz-campaign], [Find your 100][tnz-find-your-100].

- `Emotion before information`  
  Global best practice sells how a place makes you feel before it lists what is there.  
  Tourism New Zealand now frames the destination around being `connected, present, inspired and fulfilled`, then supports that with planners and toolkits. Sources: [Tourism New Zealand campaign launch][tnz-campaign], [Find your 100][tnz-find-your-100].

- `Distinctive memory devices`  
  The strongest campaigns create ownable symbols, tones, or characters.  
  Tourism Australia’s `Come and Say G’day` uses Ruby the kangaroo and recognisable Australian talent; Iceland’s `OutHorse Your Email` turns a national quirk into global earned-media bait. Sources: [Tourism Australia campaign][tourism-australia], [Inspired by Iceland OutHorse][iceland-outhorse].

- `Authenticity and local culture, not generic superlatives`  
  The best destination brands now push culture, people and lived experience, not just “beautiful scenery.”  
  Tourism New Zealand explicitly says the trip is about more than landscapes and landmarks, and highlights cultural and emotional experiences. Source: [Tourism New Zealand campaign launch][tnz-campaign].

- `Purpose and stewardship`  
  Successful place brands increasingly connect promotion with care for place.  
  California’s destination stewardship plan says a brand must activate beyond advertising and align with audience values in authentic ways. New Zealand’s Tiaki Promise makes visitor care part of the brand itself. Sources: [California stewardship plan][visitcalifornia-stewardship], [Tiaki Promise][tiaki].

- `Strong partner systems behind the creative`  
  The best brands do not just publish ads; they give operators, agents, media and partners toolkits, asset libraries and planning tools.  
  Visit California maintains a large asset library and shared brand guidance; Tourism New Zealand provides planners, videos, webinars, sales tools and a visual library; Inspired by Iceland is explicitly a public-private platform. Sources: [Visit California asset library][visitcalifornia-library], [Visit California corporate guidelines][visitcalifornia-guidelines], [TNZ travel trade][tnz-trade], [Find your 100][tnz-find-your-100], [Inspired by Iceland about][iceland-about].

- `Campaigns that flow into itineraries and bookings`  
  Best practice is not “watch film, the end.” It is film plus route-planning, themed itineraries, trade support and regional dispersal.  
  VisitBritain’s screen-tourism campaign sends people into themed itineraries and digital maps, aiming to broaden itineraries and increase length of stay. Tourism New Zealand pairs its campaign with an experience planner and trade tools. Sources: [VisitBritain screen campaign][visitbritain-screen], [Tourism New Zealand campaign launch][tnz-campaign], [Find your 100][tnz-find-your-100].

- `Regional variety under one master brand`  
  The best larger destinations make very different places feel like expressions of one coherent identity.  
  Visit California talks explicitly about a unified voice for a diverse industry and says partners should see themselves reflected in the work. Source: [Visit California corporate guidelines][visitcalifornia-guidelines].

- `Localized execution by market`  
  Strong destination brands keep the core idea consistent but vary faces, channels and creatives by audience and market.  
  Tourism Australia says it is using local talent and market-specific executions in the US, UK, China, India and Japan to connect more strongly with travellers in each market. Source: [Tourism Australia campaign][tourism-australia].

- `Resident legitimacy matters`  
  The best place marketing is designed to be something locals recognise as true and worth sharing.  
  VisitScotland has recently highlighted the contribution tourism makes to local lives through real stories, and its broader reporting links marketing with sustainable regional exploration and industry collaboration. Sources: [VisitScotland annual report][visitscotland-annual], [More Than Just A Holiday][visitscotland-more-than-just].

## 9. What that means for Lancashire

If Lancashire wants world-class place marketing, the county should copy the structure of the best brands, not just their polish.

- `Lead with one ownable idea`  
  Lancashire should not market itself as “a bit of everything in the North West.” That is weak.  
  The stronger route is something like: `Lancashire is the county of big skies, big character and real life lived properly` or `England’s proudest county coast-to-hill`. The exact line can change, but the principle is one emotionally loaded promise.

- `Make local pride visible`  
  The film should feel like Lancashire people believe in Lancashire, not like a council promo made for no one.  
  Show traders, dancers, walkers, fishers, market people, chefs, families, surfers, brass bands, festival crowds, mill heritage, football/rugby culture, and creators. Civic pride is more persuasive than glossy emptiness.

- `Treat Lancashire’s contrasts as the superpower`  
  World-class destination brands often win by turning variety into a clear narrative.  
  Lancashire has an unusually strong combination of coast, bay, hills, moors, heritage towns, food, industrial story, arts, and entertainment. That should be presented as one county with range, not as disconnected subregions.

- `Build around a few unforgettable mnemonics`  
  Lancashire needs recurring visual anchors the audience remembers after the film ends.  
  The best candidates are: Pendle Hill, the Singing Ringing Tree, Morecambe Bay sunsets, Blackpool Tower, Lytham Windmill, Clitheroe Castle, Towneley Hall, Fleetwood fishing heritage, and the county’s mills and markets.

- `Sell more than landscape`  
  New Zealand’s current campaign is explicit that landscapes alone are not enough, and that is correct.  
  Lancashire should deliberately mix scenery with food, nightlife, festivals, markets, arts, ballroom glamour, wildlife, and day-to-day human warmth.

- `Add a stewardship layer`  
  Lancashire can feel modern and responsible by framing pride as care: care for coast, moorland, mill heritage, wildlife, market towns, and communities.  
  That makes the film feel contemporary and globally credible, not just nostalgic.

- `Create a reusable partner asset system`  
  World-class place brands make it easy for partners to market consistently.  
  Lancashire should have a shared footage/stills library, pre-cleared edit selects, short social cutdowns, thematic shotlists, partner captions, and simple brand guidance that local councils, venues, festivals, and businesses can all reuse.

- `Think in campaign ecosystem terms, not one hero film`  
  The hero film should be only one output.  
  Also build:
  a food-and-drink cut,
  a coast cut,
  a Pennine/Landscape cut,
  a heritage/industry cut,
  short vertical reels,
  themed itineraries,
  and PR hooks around screen tourism, walking, coastal escapes and major events.

- `Give people a next step`  
  Best practice marketing does not leave inspiration hanging.  
  Every film or campaign should feed into actual trip ideas: weekend in Ribble Valley, coast-and-bay itinerary, Pendle and Pennines route, markets-and-mills trail, family coast break, food weekend, heritage-halls route.

- `Tailor by audience`  
  Lancashire should not use the same edit for everyone.  
  Residents need pride and recognition. Domestic visitors need ease, value and wow-factor. International audiences need iconic imagery, story clarity, and simple reasons to choose Lancashire within Britain.

- `Use proof, not just adjectives`  
  World-class destination marketing uses evidence.  
  Lancashire can legitimately say tourism is a major county industry, cite recent visitor-economy scale, and show that pride is backed by real traction, jobs, events and visitor demand. Sources: [STEAM 2024][steam-2024], [Visitor attendance 2023/24 PDF][visitor-attendance].

  Source: [Lytham Festival About][lytham-festival].

- `AIG Women's Open at Royal Lytham & St Annes`
  Dates: `July 29 to August 2, 2026`.
  Why it matters: international sport, premium hospitality, strong aerials, premium-estuary positioning, and a more polished leisure-economy story.
  Source: [AIG Women's Open 2026][aig-2026].

- `Eden Project Morecambe visible build year`
  Current source note: Eden Project Morecambe secured planning approval in February 2026, with its `Bring Me Sunshine` Chelsea Flower Show garden due to relocate to Morecambe in late summer 2026.
  Why it matters: future-facing regeneration story, national PR, and a way to show Morecambe as a place changing with confidence rather than only as nostalgia.
  Sources: [Eden planning milestone][eden-milestone], [Bring Me Sunshine Garden][eden-garden].

- `Red Rose Awards`
  Date: `March 12, 2026`.
  Why it matters: less of a public tourism event than the items above, but useful as a county-confidence and business-proofs moment if the wider project also wants an enterprise/economic-development layer.
  Source: [Red Rose Awards 2026][red-rose-2026].

- `National Festival of Making`
  Current source note: Blackburn with Darwen public papers continue to describe it as a festival of national significance, but I did not surface a public 2026 event page with confirmed dates in this pass.
  Why it matters: one of the strongest maker/industry/culture signals in the wider Lancashire brand story.
  Source: [Blackburn with Darwen public papers][festival-of-making-papers].

### 10.2 Biggest 2027 hooks

- `Tour de France through Lancashire`
  Date: `July 3, 2027`.
  Current source note: Marketing Lancashire says around `80 miles` of Stage 2 is expected to run through Lancashire via Carnforth, Morecambe, Lancaster, Forest of Bowland, Clitheroe, Langho/Whalley, Blackburn, Chorley and West Lancashire.
  Why it matters: major global visibility, very strong cycling and landscape footage, and a rare chance to market Lancashire to a mass international audience in real time.
  Sources: [Tour de France 2027 news][tour-2027-news], [Visit Lancashire Tour page][tour-2027-visit].

- `Tour de France Femmes avec Zwift across Lancashire`
  Date: `July 30, 2027`.
  Current source note: Visit Lancashire says the race will cross Lancashire on its way from Leeds to Manchester.
  Why it matters: Lancashire gets a second cycling window and a stronger case for summer 2027 being planned as a full campaign season, not a one-off race weekend.
  Source: [Visit Lancashire Tour page][tour-2027-visit].

- `Bay International Film Festival`
  Dates: `January 29 to February 7, 2027`.
  Why it matters: Morecambe cultural confidence, international guest draw, screen-tourism logic, and strong overlap with `The Bay`/coastal storytelling.
  Source: [Bay International Film Festival 2027 dates][bay-film-2027].

- `Baylight return`
  Current source note: Baylight's official site says the festival will take a break in 2026 and return in 2027.
  Why it matters: coast + light + public art + family audiences + place-making. It is one of the best civic-pride event formats Lancashire has.
  Sources: [Baylight][baylight], [Baylight pause / return][baylight-return].

- `Eden Project Morecambe free public gardens`
  Current source note: Eden says the relocated Chelsea garden and wider community gardens are due to open to the public in `spring 2027`, ahead of the main attraction's later opening.
  Why it matters: a tangible, visitable, photogenic pre-opening story and a strong regeneration narrative.
  Sources: [Eden planning milestone][eden-milestone], [Bring Me Sunshine Garden][eden-garden].

### 10.3 Strategic reading of the calendar

- `2026` is strong for food, coast, glamour, music, golf, business confidence and future-facing place change.
- `2027` is strongest for global attention, cycling, Morecambe/Bay momentum and county-scale PR.
- As of `March 20, 2026`, the public 2027 calendar is still thinner than 2026 outside cycling and the Bay/Morecambe stories.
- That means Lancashire should build an internal campaign grid now and keep dropping confirmed events into it as they publish, rather than waiting for a complete 2027 public events calendar.
