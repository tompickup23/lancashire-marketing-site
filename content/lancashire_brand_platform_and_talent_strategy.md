# Lancashire Brand Platform And Talent Strategy

Prepared on March 20, 2026.

This document is the strategic layer for the wider Lancashire marketing project. It builds on:

- `lancashire_civic_pride_footage_research.md`
- `lancashire_county_council_division_footage_breakdown.md`
- `burnley-social-following-research-2026-03-17.md`

The aim is to define a brand direction strong enough to guide films, creator work, ambassador outreach, tourism promotion, and wider place-marketing.

## 1. Executive view

The strongest route is not to invent a completely new Lancashire identity from scratch.

Marketing Lancashire already has two useful foundations:

- `Make our Lancashire yours`
- an existing `Lancashire Ambassadors` structure

The right move is to sharpen and operationalise that rather than replace it.

Recommended strategic position:

- Keep `Make our Lancashire yours` as the public invitation.
- Build the campaign around a deeper emotional truth:
  Lancashire is a county of `big character, real warmth, and proper substance`.
- Use celebrities, chefs, athletes, entrepreneurs and creators as `proof`, not as the brand itself.

In other words:

- The brand is `Lancashire`.
- The feeling is `pride, belonging, warmth, scale and surprise`.
- Talent is the mechanism that makes that believable.

## 2. What the official Lancashire material already tells us

Marketing Lancashire is already pointing in the right direction.

- The `Make our Lancashire yours` toolkit says the campaign should use the voices of real people who live and work in the county, convey Lancashire's contrast of `country, coastline and cities`, and put `people at its centre`, with welcome and warmth as core cues. Source: [Make our Lancashire yours toolkit](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/app/uploads/2022/11/Make-our-Lancashire-yours-campaign-toolkit-22.pdf).
- The Lancashire Ambassadors manifesto says ambassadors are committed to act as `inspirational voices of Lancashire`, are `Lancashire invested and proud`, and should connect people and organisations to Lancashire roots. Source: [Lancashire Ambassadors Manifesto](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/app/uploads/2023/03/Lancashire-Ambassadors-Manifesto-1.pdf).
- Marketing Lancashire's visitor-economy growth plan says the challenge is that individual destinations are known better than the county as a whole, and Lancashire often gets buried at the bottom of an address or not referenced at all. It says the task is to `connect the individual place assets in Lancashire to the county that they are in`, while activating `pride in place`. Source: [Building Better Demand](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/app/uploads/2023/02/Building-Better-Demand-A-Visitor-Economy-Growth-Plan-Consultation-Draft-For-Circulation-.pdf).
- The same growth plan says Lancashire's competitive strengths include `coast`, `seaside resorts`, `market towns`, `a historic city`, `food and drink`, `heritage and industrial heritage`, `outdoor activities`, and major catalysts like Blackpool and Morecambe. Source: [Building Better Demand](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/app/uploads/2023/02/Building-Better-Demand-A-Visitor-Economy-Growth-Plan-Consultation-Draft-For-Circulation-.pdf).

That gives us a very clear brief:

- unify the county story
- connect the famous places to the Lancashire name
- keep people and pride at the centre
- build a modern county narrative, not a nostalgic brochure

## 3. The brand problem

Lancashire has an unusually strong product but a weaker county-level mental picture than it should.

People often know:

- Blackpool
- Morecambe
- Lancaster
- Lytham
- Pendle
- Ribble Valley

But they do not always connect those places into one coherent county idea.

That is exactly the gap the official growth plan identifies.

The branding challenge is therefore not:

- `How do we make Lancashire look nice?`

It is:

- `How do we make Lancashire feel unified, memorable, modern and worth caring about?`

## 4. The brand opportunity

Lancashire has a rare combination of qualities that most destinations would kill for:

- iconic coast
- cinematic uplands
- industrial and cultural heft
- proper food credibility
- famous entertainment heritage
- strong sporting roots
- market-town and city contrast
- local warmth that does not feel manufactured

The winning story is not luxury escapism.

The winning story is:

- `Lancashire is where real life is lived brilliantly.`

That can hold together:

- civic pride
- tourism
- inward investment
- events
- culture
- social content

## 5. Recommended brand core

## 5.1 Brand promise

Lancashire offers visitors and residents something more substantial than a generic break: a county full of character, contrast, warmth and stories that feel lived rather than staged.

## 5.2 Brand essence

`Big character. Real warmth. Proper Lancashire.`

This is not proposed as the public-facing slogan. It is the internal creative test.

If a piece of work does not feel like all three, it is probably drifting.

## 5.3 Public-facing master line

Recommended:

- `Make our Lancashire yours`

Reason:

- it already exists
- it is participatory rather than boastful
- it can work for residents, visitors, ambassadors, partners and creators
- it leaves room for many different stories under one umbrella

## 5.4 Supporting campaign line options

These are creative routes, not formal recommendations to replace the master line.

- `Big character. Proper welcome.`
- `This is Lancashire. Make it yours.`
- `More county. More character.`
- `Proud to be from. Worth travelling for.`

Of these, the safest and strongest pairing is:

- public line: `Make our Lancashire yours`
- internal creative line: `Big character. Real warmth. Proper Lancashire.`

## 6. Brand personality

Lancashire should sound and feel:

- warm
- grounded
- witty
- direct
- unpretentious
- generous
- confident
- substantial

Lancashire should not sound:

- try-hard luxury
- soft-focus generic wellness
- fake-posh
- over-scripted
- tourist-board bland

The best tone is human and proud, with a little bite and texture.

## 7. Brand pillars

These are the repeatable pillars every major campaign should draw from.

### 7.1 Character

Lancashire is full of strong personalities, stories, accents, makers, fans, chefs, performers, entrepreneurs and communities.

This is the pillar that stops the work feeling generic.

### 7.2 Contrast

The official toolkit already leans into `country, coastline and cities`. That is right.

Add:

- mills and moors
- castles and comedy
- ballroom glamour and wild weather
- markets and Michelin stars

### 7.3 Warmth

Lancashire marketing should feel hospitable rather than performative.

The county's advantage is that warmth looks believable here.

### 7.4 Substance

Lancashire should feel economically, culturally and historically weighty.

That means showing:

- heritage
- enterprise
- visitor scale
- quality food
- cultural production
- living communities

### 7.5 Pride

Pride should be visible, not just narrated.

Show:

- supporters
- local rituals
- makers at work
- hometown references
- multigenerational use of place

## 8. Audience logic

One film cannot do every job.

The brand should be stable, but the emphasis should shift by audience.

### 8.1 Residents

Need:

- recognition
- respect
- truthfulness
- pride without cringe

Message:

- `This county has depth, talent, history and a future worth backing.`

### 8.2 Domestic leisure visitors

Need:

- wow-factor
- easy reasons to choose Lancashire
- confidence that the county is varied and good value

Message:

- `You can get coast, countryside, culture, food and personality in one county.`

### 8.3 International and out-of-region audiences

Need:

- iconic imagery
- simplified county narrative
- clear sense of what is distinctive

Message:

- `Lancashire is one of England's most varied and characterful counties.`

### 8.4 Partners and stakeholders

Need:

- a shared language
- a way to align their own assets to the county story

Message:

- `Your place, business or attraction becomes stronger when it is visibly part of Lancashire.`

## 9. Visual doctrine

The film and campaign assets should follow a few hard rules.

### 9.1 Put people in the frame

World-class destination marketing does not rely only on empty scenery.

Lancashire should show:

- faces
- movement
- crowds
- making
- cheering
- cooking
- walking
- dancing

### 9.2 Use mnemonic icons repeatedly

The audience should leave remembering a few strong symbols.

Top county mnemonics:

- Pendle Hill
- Singing Ringing Tree
- Morecambe Bay sunsets
- Blackpool Tower
- Lytham Windmill
- Clitheroe Castle
- Towneley Hall
- mills and markets

### 9.3 Cut contrast hard

Do not group all the coast together, then all the hills, then all the towns.

Cut between opposites so the county feels rich and surprising.

### 9.4 Keep texture

Include:

- weather
- brick
- stone
- steel
- water
- tramlines
- market stalls
- food prep
- football scarves
- brass and applause

Texture is what makes place real.

## 10. Talent strategy principle

The key rule is simple:

- `Talent must amplify the county proposition, not distract from it.`

That means:

- no generic celebrity montage
- no random famous faces with weak county connection
- no endorsements detached from place

Every talent appearance should answer:

- why this person
- why Lancashire
- why this place
- why now

### 11.4 Tier 4: Local creator network

This is where the social media star research matters.

Lancashire should copy the logic of VisitScotland's `community creators`, `storytelling creators` and `editorial creators`, rather than relying only on celebrity.

Best-practice rationale:

- VisitScotland says social audiences want authentic, first-hand perspectives and has built a Content Creator Network with local b-roll creators, short-form storytellers, and longer-form editorial creators.
- VisitScotland also says all creator output should align to one message framework.

Sources:

- [VisitScotland: Telling Scotland's Story On Camera](https://www.visitscotland.org/news/2025/content-creator-network)
- [VisitScotland influencer and content creator programmes](https://www.visitscotland.org/who-we-are/contact/influencer-programmes)

Recommended Lancashire creator structure:

- `Community creators`
  Local photographers, short-form shooters, and place-based micro-creators who capture everyday Lancashire life.

- `Storytelling creators`
  Short-form video creators who can build emotionally engaging place narratives for TikTok, Reels and Shorts.

- `Editorial creators`
  Higher-production filmmakers or YouTubers who can make hosted day-out, itinerary and documentary-style features.

Match person to place.

## 14. What to avoid

### 14.1 Do not use talent as wallpaper

If a celebrity only appears to say "Lancashire is great", that is weak.

Make them tell a place-rooted story.

### 14.2 Do not over-index on one town

Burnley matters, Blackpool matters, Preston matters, Morecambe matters, Lancaster matters, Ribble Valley matters.

The county story has to feel county-sized.

### 14.3 Do not let football tribalism narrow the county

Football can be part of the texture, but the county brand must be broader than club allegiance.

### 14.4 Do not sound like a local-authority advert

The work should feel culturally confident, not bureaucratic.

## 15. Suggested campaign system

The strongest Lancashire brand system would look like this:

- `Hero film`
  County-scale emotional statement.

- `County cutdowns`
  30s, 15s, 6s versions.

- `Place stories`
  Morecambe Bay, Blackpool, Burnley/Pendle, Preston, Ribble Valley, Rossendale, West Lancashire.

- `Talent stories`
  Creator and ambassador-led pieces rooted in home places.

- `Taste stories`
  Producers, markets, chefs, ingredients, hospitality.

- `Proper Lancashire moments`
  User-generated or creator-shot clips of everyday county life, edited under one clear style.

## 16. The best deep branding direction for this project

If I had to lock the direction now, I would recommend:

- Keep `Make our Lancashire yours`
- Define Lancashire internally as `big character, real warmth, proper substance`
- Build the hero film around place and people equally
- Use a small number of high-trust county faces
- Build a formal creator network behind the scenes
- Use food, culture, coast, landscape and enterprise as equal proof points

That is a much stronger and more durable route than either of these weaker alternatives:

- `pure scenic montage`
- `random celebrity endorsement reel`

- a `community representation` layer

## 19. Lancashire-branded products and symbolic economy

World-class place brands do not live only in ads.

They live in:

- what people drink
- what they eat
- what they gift
- what sits on menus and shelves
- what appears in farm shops, magazines and hotel rooms

This is one of Lancashire's underused strengths.

### 19.1 Why products matter

Lancashire should not just market places.

It should market an `everyday county culture`:

- tea in the cupboard
- crisps in the deli
- cheese in the hamper
- sauce on the table
- hotpot on the menu
- Lancashire media on the coffee table

That is how a county brand becomes lived rather than merely advertised.

### 19.2 Strongest Lancashire-branded product signals

- `Lancashire Tea`
  Why: an everyday ritual product is extremely useful in hospitality, gifting, merch, hamper content, accommodation welcome packs and business events.
  Current source note: Lancashire Tea says the brand is a Keith Spicer Ltd trademark and gives a Blackburn address.
  Source: [Lancashire Tea contact page](https://shop.lancashiretea.co.uk/pages/contact-us), [Lancashire Tea about page](https://shop.lancashiretea.co.uk/pages/about-us).
  Division note: `outside Lancashire County Council divisions`, because Blackburn with Darwen is a unitary authority rather than Lancashire County Council geography.

- `Lancashire hotpot`
  Why: probably the most emotionally legible county dish. It is warm, recognisable, seasonal and works brilliantly in film because it connects food, family, pubs, autumn/winter, and county tradition without feeling fake-posh.
  Source: [Visit Lancashire - Nigel Haworth's Lancashire Hotpot](https://www.visitlancashire.com/dbimgs/nigel-haworth-lancashire-hotpot.pdf).
  Division note: `county-wide rather than single-division`.

- `Lancashire Life`
  Why: this is not a food product, but it is still a county-branded consumer object and a useful cultural proof point. It shows that Lancashire already exists in the market as a lifestyle and editorial identity.
  Source: [Lancashire Life / Great British Life](https://www.greatbritishlife.co.uk/magazines/lancashire/).
  Division note: `county-wide`.

- `Fiddler's Lancashire Crisps`
  Why: one of the best examples of a modern Lancashire product system. The brand explicitly ties potatoes, Lancashire ingredients and local pride together, and is inherently usable in farm-shop, deli, hospitality and gifting content.
  Source: [Fiddler's Lancashire Crisps](https://www.fiddlerslancashirecrisps.co.uk/).
  Division note: `Burscough & Rufford`.

- `Mrs Kirkham's Lancashire Cheese`
  Why: premium, artisanal, highly legible, and tied to actual landscape and farming identity rather than a vague county label.
  Source: [Mrs Kirkham's Cheese](https://www.mrskirkhamscheese.co.uk/).
  Division note: `Preston Rural`.

- `Lancashire Bomb / premium Lancashire cheese gifting`
  Why: Lancashire needs giftable premium shorthand. Lancashire Bomb-style cheese is one of the most recognisable examples and works well in hampers, Christmas, hospitality and retail storytelling.
  Division note: `Goosnargh / Preston Rural orbit`.

- `Lancashire Sauce`
  Why: distinctive, funny, regional and full of character. It is exactly the kind of product that helps a county feel like a place with its own taste and attitude.
  Current source note: Lancashire Sauce says the story began in a chip shop in `Padiham Lancashire`.
  Source: [Lancashire Sauce](https://www.lancashiresauce.co.uk/).
  Division note: `Padiham and Burnley West` by origin story.

- `Beacon Fell Traditional Lancashire Cheese`
  Why: protected-food credibility gives Lancashire cultural and economic substance. It links produce, place, landscape and authenticity in one move.
  Source: [GOV.UK - Beacon Fell Traditional Lancashire Cheese](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/beacon-fell-traditional-lancashire-cheese-non-minor-amendment-opportunity-to-object).
  Division note: strongest activation territory is `Preston Rural`, with spill into `Longridge with Bowland` and nearby Ribble Valley/Bowland-facing areas.

Strategic use:

- feature Lancashire-branded products in hotel welcome shots, hamper gifting, farm-shop content, chef films, markets, menus, and creator reels
- treat them as `proof of county culture`, not as random props
- build retail, gifting and hospitality partnerships around them so the campaign becomes tangible

Lancashire now has a meaningful run of hooks. The job is not to list them once; it is to sequence them into a campaign rhythm.

### 20.1 2026 anchors

- `Red Rose Awards`
  Why it matters: this is a business-confidence and county-enterprise proof point, useful for inward investment, civic pride and stakeholder-facing content.
  Date: `March 12, 2026`.
  Source: [Marketing Lancashire - Red Rose Awards 2026 finalists](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/news/red-rose-awards-2026-finalists-are-named/).
  Division note: `outside Lancashire County Council divisions` because Winter Gardens Blackpool is in unitary Blackpool.

- `Blackpool Dance Festival 100th Anniversary`
  Why it matters: almost no counties have a heritage-glamour event of this recognisable international stature. It is a major tourism and identity asset, even though it sits outside Lancashire County Council geography.
  Dates: `May 16 to May 29, 2026`.
  Source: [Blackpool Dance Festival 100th Anniversary](https://blackpooldancefestival.com/festivals/blackpool-dance-festival/).
  Division note: `outside Lancashire County Council divisions`.

- `Lytham Festival 15th Anniversary`
  Why it matters: big crowd pull, strong sponsor and media value, and very effective coast-meets-culture imagery.
  Current source note: the festival says 2026 will be its `15th Anniversary` and that it now attracts `well in excess of 120,000 people`.
  Source: [Lytham Festival - About Us](https://www.lythamfestival.com/about-us).
  Division note: `Lytham`.

- `AIG Women's Open at Royal Lytham & St Annes`
  Why it matters: genuine international sports prestige, premium audience, hospitality value and very strong aerial/estuary/links-golf visuals.
  Dates: `July 29 to August 2, 2026`.
  Source: [AIG Women's Open - Royal Lytham & St Annes 2026](https://www.aigwomensopen.com/royal-lytham-st-annes-2026).
  Division note: `St Annes South`.

- `Eden Project Morecambe visible build year`
  Why it matters: this is one of the biggest future-facing Lancashire stories. Eden Project Morecambe secured planning approval in February 2026, with the first visible public-facing elements landing before the main opening.
  Current source note: Eden says relocation of the Chelsea `Bring Me Sunshine` garden begins in late summer 2026, ahead of free public gardens opening in spring 2027.
  Sources: [Eden Project Morecambe planning milestone](https://www.edenproject.com/eden-project-morecambe-celebrates-a-major-milestone-paving-the-way-to-break-ground-in-2026), [Chelsea Flower Show 2026 / Bring Me Sunshine Garden](https://www.edenproject.com/new-edens/eden-project-morecambe-uk/chelsea-flower-show-2026).
  Division note: `Morecambe Central`.

- `Northcote's prestige food calendar`
  Why it matters: Lancashire's premium food story needs recurring high-end proof, not just one annual headline. Northcote gives that.
  Current source note: Northcote's live 2026 events calendar is active and the site continues to position `Obsession Festival` as a major culinary property.
  Source: [Northcote - What's on](https://www.northcote.com/whats-on-at-northcote/).
  Division note: `Ribble Valley South West`.

- `National Festival of Making`
  Why it matters: one of the strongest maker/industry/culture proof points in the wider Lancashire story.
  Current source note: Blackburn with Darwen public papers continue to describe it as a festival of national significance, but I did not surface a public 2026 event page with confirmed dates in this pass.
  Source: [Blackburn with Darwen public papers](https://blackburn.moderngov.co.uk/documents/g4168/Public%20reports%20pack%2004th-Dec-2025%2018.00%20Policy%20Council.pdf?T=10).
  Division note: `outside Lancashire County Council divisions`.

### 20.2 2027 anchors

- `Tour de France through Lancashire`
  Why it matters: this is one of the biggest global visibility moments Lancashire has had in years.
  Date: `July 3, 2027`.
  Current source note: Marketing Lancashire says around `80 miles` of Stage 2 is expected to run through Lancashire, including Carnforth, Morecambe, Lancaster, Forest of Bowland, Clitheroe, Langho/Whalley, Blackburn, Chorley and West Lancashire.
  Sources: [Marketing Lancashire - Tour de France 2027](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/news/world-class-cyclists-heading-to-lancashire-for-tour-de-france-2027/), [Visit Lancashire - Tour de France GB 2027](https://www.visitlancashire.com/whats-on/tour-de-france-gb-2027-p1162190).

Exact division-level routeing will need confirming when the final road book is published.

  Why it matters: this doubles the cycling story and gives Lancashire two national/global cycling windows in the same month.
  Date: `July 30, 2027`.
  Current source note: Visit Lancashire says the race will start in Leeds and cross Lancashire on its way to Manchester.
  Source: [Visit Lancashire - Tour de France GB 2027](https://www.visitlancashire.com/whats-on/tour-de-france-gb-2027-p1162190).
  Division note: exact Lancashire division mapping is not yet specific enough to lock down from current public route information.

- `Bay International Film Festival`
  Why it matters: a very useful cultural-confidence story for Morecambe, with international guests and genuine local/community participation.
  Dates: `January 29 to February 7, 2027`.
  Source: [Visit Lancashire - Bay International Film Festival 2027 dates](https://www.visitlancashire.com/blog/read/2026/02/rave-reviews-for-film-festival-as-it-announces-2027-dates-b2665).

- `Baylight return`
  Why it matters: this is one of Morecambe's strongest place-making events because it combines coast, public art, family participation and direct economic value.
  Current source note: Baylight's official site says the festival will take a break in 2026 and return in 2027.
  Sources: [Baylight Morecambe](https://baylightmorecambe.co.uk/), [Visit Lancashire - Baylight pause until 2027](https://www.visitlancashire.com/blog/read/2025/07/baylight-festival-takes-a-pause-but-morecambes-light-will-shine-brighter-in-2027-b2460), [Lancashire County Council Baylight support news](https://news.lancashire.gov.uk/news/morecambes-baylight-festival-to-return-in-2025-following-council-intervention).
  Division note: strongest visual anchors are `Morecambe Central`, with promenade spill into `Morecambe North` and `Morecambe South`.

- `Eden Project Morecambe free public gardens`
  Why it matters: a very strong pre-opening story that can be marketed before the full Eden opening in 2028. It turns anticipation into a real visitable asset.
  Current source note: Eden says the relocated Chelsea garden and wider community gardens are due to open to the public in `spring 2027`.
  Sources: [Eden Project Morecambe planning milestone](https://www.edenproject.com/eden-project-morecambe-celebrates-a-major-milestone-paving-the-way-to-break-ground-in-2026), [Bring Me Sunshine Garden](https://www.edenproject.com/new-edens/eden-project-morecambe-uk/chelsea-flower-show-2026).
  Division note: `Morecambe Central`.

### 20.3 Calendar discipline

The important strategic point is this:

- 2026 should be used to build `food + coast + glamour + future Lancashire`
- 2027 should be used to build `global attention + cycling + bay regeneration + county confidence`

As of `March 20, 2026`, confirmed 2027 public programming is still thinner than 2026 outside the cycling and Morecambe stories.

That means Lancashire should build an internal 2027 comms grid now and keep adding confirmed events as they publish, rather than waiting for a perfect public calendar to appear.
