# Lancashire County Hero Film Script

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This is the first `production-ready county hero film` document for Lancashire.

It turns the county strategy into a real edit brief:

- one `100 second` master film
- one clear emotional story
- one timecoded footage map
- one voiceover script
- one rule-set for quality, music and pacing

Use this with:

- the county strategy
- the borough strategy
- the footage quality assessment
- the division breakdown

## 1. Best-practice rules used

This script is built around the strongest recurring rules from current destination-brand and tourism-film research.

### 1.1 One hero feeling, not a list of attractions

The film should be built around one emotional proposition:

- `warmth with weight behind it`

That is the strongest Lancashire answer to current best practice:

- `Destination NSW` explicitly moved from a traditional destination brand into a `feelings-led` and `culture-brand` approach, with each film aligned to a `single hero feeling`.[^dnsw-feel-new]
- `VisitScotland` says the promise of an `emotional experience` is a critical differentiator and that stories are what create connection and enrichment.[^visitscotland-soul]
- recent tourism-video research found that `creativity and originality` drive `positive emotional reactions`, which in turn improve attitudes to both the film and the destination.[^mdpi-emovie]

### 1.2 Go beyond postcard icons

Icons matter, but top-tier destination films do not stop at postcard imagery.

- `Destination NSW` says the Sydney campaign should `look beyond the postcard attractions` and reveal a side of the place audiences have `never seen or felt before`.[^dnsw-sydney]
- `VisitBritain` says creative work should challenge cliché, find `new, surprising stories`, and use `icons with a twist`.[^visitbritain-foundations]

That means Lancashire should use `Morecambe Bay`, `Pendle`, `Blackpool` and `Clitheroe`, but quickly move into people, markets, makers, food, culture and future industry.

### 1.3 Put people in the frame early

High-quality place films feel inhabited.

- `Tourism New Zealand` says people considering a trip want not just scenery, but connection with `people, places and culture` and reassurance about how welcoming locals are.[^tnz-a-nz]
- `VisitBritain` photography guidance warns against imagery that feels `staged`, `flat`, lacking energy, or missing people altogether.[^visitbritain-photo]
- `VisitScotland` says today's travellers trust `real stories, told by real people`.[^visitscotland-storytelling]

So this film should show faces, use, movement and public life by `0:20`, not save humanity for the end.

### 1.4 Use a narrative arc, not a montage pile

The county film should feel like a short journey:

- `discovery`
- `belonging`
- `proof`
- `future`
- `invitation`

Academic work on destination-brand films says storytelling is still underused, and that stronger narrative structure is a missed opportunity for many DMOs.[^storytelling-videos]

### 1.5 Music must be modular and place-specific

The film should not use generic trailer music.

- `Destination NSW` built bespoke remixes of its core soundtrack to match the feeling of each film.[^dnsw-feel-new]
- `Tourism New Zealand` describes its model as a `core brand narrative + mindset based modular content` and stresses bold, emotive work that scales across channels.[^tnz-soi]

For Lancashire, that means:

- one master theme
- subtle `Wurlitzer` colour
- warm choir or communal vocal lift
- modern pulse
- alternate stems for borough and shorter-cut versions

### 1.6 Business proof must be exact and brief

The county film needs economic substance, but it should come as punctuation, not corporate overload.

The strongest model is:

- emotional destination film first
- exact proof inserts second

That is why `BAE Warton`, `Samlesbury / AMRC`, `Rolls-Royce Barnoldswick` and `Eden Project Morecambe` appear in this film as short, exact and attributable beats rather than long explanatory sections.

### 1.7 Every shot must either deepen feeling or prove truth

If a shot is only pretty, it should be cut.

The county hero film should only use shots that do at least one of these jobs:

- expand Lancashire emotionally
- make it feel more lived in
- prove quality
- prove modern relevance
- close the invitation

## 2. Film spec

- `Runtime`
  `100 seconds`

- `Master format`
  `16:9`, cinematic master for web, presentations and event playback

- `Derived edits`
  `60 second`, `30 second`, `15 second`, silent texted socials, borough-specific cut-ins

- `Voiceover style`
  Warm, grounded, assured, not theatrical and not tourist-board cheesy

- `Music direction`
  Felt piano and atmosphere in the first `10 seconds`, then strings and subtle Wurlitzer colour, then percussion pulse, then choir lift, then a broad final resolve

- `Edit rule`
  No more than `two empty scenic shots` back-to-back

- `Human-presence rule`
  First recognisable human or lived-in public scene by `0:20`

- `Business-proof rule`
  First exact future-economy proof by `1:05`

- `Text rule`
  Keep on-screen text minimal
  Use only:
  `Lancashire` at the end
  `Make our Lancashire yours` as the final line

## 3. Full voiceover script

`This is not a county that asks to be admired from a distance.`

`It wants to be felt.`

`In the bay wind.`

`In the climb.`

`In the first light on stone, steel and sea.`

`In ballrooms, market squares and harbour mornings.`

`In food worth gathering for, and streets that still carry their people with pride.`

`Lancashire is made by makers.`

`By dancers, traders, singers, walkers and builders.`

`By old knowledge and new ambition.`

`By halls, hills, airfields, workshops and coasts that keep moving forward.`

`So if you think you know this county, look again.`

`There is more warmth here.`

`More character.`

`More future.`

`Make our Lancashire yours.`

## 4. Edit structure

The film should move in five acts.

| Act | Time | Job | Emotional effect |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `1` | `0:00 to 0:20` | Open on scale and reset assumptions | Awe and curiosity |
| `2` | `0:20 to 0:50` | Move into lived-in county life | Warmth and belonging |
| `3` | `0:50 to 1:05` | Prove taste, craft and cultural depth | Pride and texture |
| `4` | `1:05 to 1:25` | Prove future momentum and economic seriousness | Confidence |
| `5` | `1:25 to 1:40` | Resolve emotionally and invite the viewer in | Lift and ownership |

## 6. Edit logic by act

### 6.1 Act one: reset the audience

The first `20 seconds` should do one thing:

- break the lazy assumption that Lancashire is just one more northern county montage

That is why the opening order is:

1. `bay`
2. `hill`
3. `castle`
4. `ballroom`

It says:

- scale
- symbolism
- heritage
- glamour

before the audience has time to file Lancashire away as generic.

### 6.2 Act two: move into human Lancashire

From `0:20` onward, the film should feel used, inhabited and social.

That is why the middle section prioritises:

- `Preston`
- `Morecambe`
- `Fleetwood`
- `Lytham`
- `Towneley`
- `Burnley / Pendle`

This is where the film stops being scenic and starts feeling civic.

### 6.3 Act three: prove taste and making

From `0:50 to 1:05`, the film needs texture:

- makers
- food
- detail
- hands
- crowds

This is the point where the audience should stop thinking `nice place` and start thinking `substantial place`.

### 6.4 Act four: puncture with future proof

From `1:05 to 1:25`, the film must show that Lancashire is still building.

The rule is:

- exact
- attributable
- brief

Do not use:

- generic jets
- abstract engine reels
- anonymous corporate corridors

Do use:

- clearly `Warton`
- clearly `Samlesbury / AMRC`
- clearly `Barnoldswick`
- clearly `Morecambe` future imagery

### 6.5 Act five: close on ownership

The last `15 seconds` should not feel like an ad signing off.

It should feel like:

- emotional release
- public energy
- belonging
- then invitation

That is why the end returns to:

- spectacle
- community
- horizon

## 7. What must be captured before picture lock

The current stack is strong enough for an offline proof cut, but not for final top quality.

These are the `must-have` upgrades before final picture lock:

- `Pendle Hill` dawn and ridge hero motion
- `Singing Ringing Tree` clean hero motion
- `Towneley Hall` reveal and public-life movement
- `Preston Harris / Flag Market / river` polished civic motion
- `Lytham Windmill and Green` polished premium-coast motion
- `Fleetwood harbour and market` polished working-coast motion
- `BAE Warton` exact approved aerospace package
- `Samlesbury / AMRC` approved future-industry package
- `Rolls-Royce Barnoldswick` or equivalent approved Pendle precision-manufacturing package
- one `county community capture day` for choir, crowd, market, family and public-life inserts

## 8. Fallback build if access footage is delayed

If the permission-led industrial footage does not clear in time, do `not` collapse into generic business stock.

Instead:

- hold the master film at `tourism + civic pride + making`
- use `Festival of Making`, market, maker, university, harbour and public-life footage more heavily
- keep `future` as a `10 second` promise rather than a `20 second` proof layer
- release the first master as `County Film V1`
- add the industry puncture later in `County Film V2`

That is a better top-quality outcome than weakening the film with generic proxy footage.

## 9. Immediate production order

1. Cut a `scratch assembly` using the current ready footage and commons pools.
2. Book `Package A`, `Package B`, `Package C` and `Package D`.
3. Send access requests for `BAE Warton`, `AMRC / Samlesbury`, `Rolls-Royce Barnoldswick`, and `Eden Project Morecambe`.
4. Commission the first `Lancashire theme` with stems for `hero`, `borough`, `social`, and `end card`.
5. Build the `100 second` master first, then derive the `60`, `30` and `15 second` edits from it.

## 10. Final standard

The county film should feel like:

- `a real place`
- `a proud place`
- `a place with emotional scale`
- `a place with weight behind the beauty`
- `a place that is still moving`

If it feels like a generic northern drone reel with a nice voiceover, it has failed.

If it feels like Lancashire has beauty, people, memory, confidence and momentum in the same breath, it is on brief.
