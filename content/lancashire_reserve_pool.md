# Lancashire Reserve Pool

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This is the `reserve pool` for the Lancashire marketing project.

Its job is to preserve:

- `Maybe` people
- second-wave borough leads
- lower-confidence event hooks
- useful but not top-tier products
- music references and discovery sources
- content leads that are interesting but not ready for the main county or borough strategy

## 1. How to use this

- if a lead is good enough for the main county strategy, move it out of here
- if a lead is strong enough for a borough strategy, use it there first
- if a lead is useful but not fully verified, keep it here with an explicit quality label

Data quality labels:

- `High`
  Strong public basis, but not currently top-tier strategy.

- `Medium`
  Defensible public basis, but still secondary or use-case-specific.

- `Low`
  Useful working lead or directional signal, but not ready for outward-facing copy.

## 8. Sensitive and non-public categories

- `Adult creator economy low-quality leads`
  Intentionally not carried into the public reserve pool.

Why:

- low-quality or directory-only adult-platform claims are too weak for a public strategy stack
- only strongly sourced, self-public, clearly adult cases should ever be retained
- even then, they belong in a tightly controlled private outreach bucket rather than the public-facing strategy architecture

## 9. Final reserve rule

This pool exists to preserve useful material without diluting the main strategy.

If a lead is:

- not strong enough for the county top line
- not clean enough for a borough strategy
- or interesting but still incomplete

it belongs here until the evidence or need improves.
