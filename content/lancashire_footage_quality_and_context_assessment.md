# Lancashire Footage Quality and Context Assessment

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This is the production-facing footage assessment layer for the Lancashire video system.

Use it after:

- the county strategy
- the borough strategy
- the main footage research pack
- the county-council division breakdown

Its job is to answer five practical questions:

- `How good is the footage we already have?`
- `How exact is it?`
- `How easy is it to use legally?`
- `What does it actually say about Lancashire in context?`
- `Is it ready for the first county and local video plans?`

## 1. Scoring system

Each source below is assessed on a simple `1 to 5` scale.

- `Tech quality`
  `5` = clean 4K/UHD or obviously polished HD motion.
  `3` = usable but mixed or still-pool heavy.
  `1` = poor or not practical for hero use.

- `Exactness`
  `5` = unmistakably exact Lancashire place, person or institution.
  `3` = exact enough but broader or patchier.
  `1` = proxy only.

- `Licence ease`
  `5` = clearly reusable free source such as Pixabay, Pexels or Commons.
  `3` = usable but needs asset-by-asset checking.
  `1` = reference or licensing lead only.

- `Story fit`
  `5` = directly advances the county story.
  `3` = useful support footage.
  `1` = weak strategic value.

- `Context risk`
  `Low` = the footage says the right thing by default.
  `Medium` = useful, but easy to misframe if overused.
  `High` = visually strong, but easy to make generic or misleading.

## 5. Context assessment rules

The current footage stack is strongest when it is used with clear intent.

- `Coast footage`
  Works best as scale, release, glamour and recognisability. On its own it can make Lancashire feel too leisure-only.

- `Hill and moorland footage`
  Works best as myth, challenge, weather and symbolic county identity. On its own it can make Lancashire feel empty.

- `Town and civic footage`
  Works best as lived-in pride, credibility and warmth. Without it, the campaign risks becoming scenic but shallow.

- `Business and innovation footage`
  Works best as a short proving layer. It should punctuate the film, not overwhelm it.

- `People footage`
  Works best when it feels rooted in place: traders, walkers, dancers, supporters, founders, chefs, young people, volunteers.

- `Archive footage`
  Works best as sparing emotional texture. It should never become the main body of the first films.

- `Preston civic and park` for public life and urban warmth
- `Burnley / Pendle commons pools` for grit, myth and East Lancashire identity

### 6.3 County proof layer

Best current proof inserts:

- one food or hospitality proxy from the main footage research pack
- one town-centre or market scene
- one permission-led business or future-industry beat such as `Eden`, `BAE`, `AMRC`, `Vital Energi` or `Festival of Making`

### 6.4 County close

Best current close options:

- `Morecambe Bay` at golden hour
- `Blackpool` at night if the close is meant to feel celebratory
- `Pendle` or `Ribble Valley` if the close is meant to feel rooted and epic

## 9. What to do next

The current system is good enough to start, but not good enough to stop.

Immediate actions:

- cut a `county hero proof-of-concept` from the current strong free motion stack
- run `three top-up shoot days`: `East Lancashire`, `Fylde / Wyre coast`, `Preston / South Ribble`
- prepare a short `permission ask list` for `Eden`, `AMRC`, `BAE`, `Vital Energi`, `Festival of Making`, `The Harris`
- use the first local pilots to test the shared typography, music stems and end-card system before rolling the system across the full division slate

## 10. Final judgement

The current footage stack is already strong enough to make:

- one compelling county teaser
- several very good first local films
- a convincing deck for stakeholders

It is `not yet` strong enough to make the final full county system feel complete without:

- fresh exact motion for `Pendle`, `Burnley`, `Fylde`, `Fleetwood`, `Preston`, and `South Ribble`
- a permission-led economic footage layer
- more human footage tied to borough identity rather than generic scenic beauty
