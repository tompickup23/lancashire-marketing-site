# Lancashire Borough Influence and Business Map

Checked on `21 March 2026`.

This is a `campaign-working shortlist`, not a definitive ranking.

For each borough / unitary area, the aim is to identify:

- `people with public reach, civic weight or commercial influence`
- `past legends, legacy icons or inherited stories that deepen place memory`
- `big, symbolically important or innovative businesses`
- `projects that make the borough look future-facing`

The scope here is the wider Lancashire brand geography, so `Blackpool` and `Blackburn with Darwen` are included.

Important use note:

- treat the `people` sections as `campaign-useful public associations`, not as a formal greatest-hits ranking
- legacy icons are included because world-class place marketing needs `historic depth` as well as current relevance
- before using an explicit birthplace claim in public-facing copy, double-check the exact wording if a figure is better known by `association` than by `birth`

## Blackburn with Darwen

- `Current influence`
  `AJ Odudu` is the cleanest current mainstream media name. In practice, Blackburn with Darwen’s present-day influence is also carried by major employers, the maker-culture ecosystem and the visibility of Blackburn Rovers.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Barbara Castle` and `Jack Walker` are the strongest shorthand figures because they give the borough political weight, industrial success and football-era memory in one move.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Vital Energi` is one of Lancashire’s major regional employers and a strong clean-energy proof point. `Lancashire Tea` is a useful county-branded consumer product. The `Blackburn Cyber Campus and Innovation Quarter` is already part of the county’s devolution-era growth story. The `National Festival of Making` is not just culture; it is also a maker-economy asset. Sources: [Lancashire large employers](https://www.lancashire.gov.uk/lancashire-insight/economy/major-employers/major-public-and-private-sector-employers/), [LCCA anniversary update](https://news.lancashire.gov.uk/news/lcca-marks-one-year-anniversary), [Lancashire Tea](https://shop.lancashiretea.co.uk/pages/about-us), [Festival of Making papers](https://blackburn.moderngov.co.uk/documents/g4168/Public%20reports%20pack%2004th-Dec-2025%2018.00%20Policy%20Council.pdf?T=10).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Outside Lancashire County Council divisions`, because Blackburn with Darwen is a unitary authority.

- `Campaign angle`
  Blackburn with Darwen gives Lancashire a `clean energy + football memory + industrial confidence + making culture` story.

## Blackpool

- `Current influence`
  `Jenna Coleman` and `Robert Smith` are useful modern cultural names. Blackpool’s real strength, though, is that the town itself already behaves like a national entertainment brand.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `George Formby`, `Reginald Dixon`, and the wider ballroom / variety / illuminations tradition give Blackpool an inherited glamour stack that few English places can match.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Silicon Sands` is the most future-facing business story, pitched around renewably powered data centres, AI-adjacent growth and heat-network potential. `Blackpool Pleasure Beach`, `Winter Gardens`, and the wider entertainment economy remain the borough’s strongest commercial icons. Sources: [Silicon Sands](https://www.blackpool.gov.uk/news/silicon-sands-masterplan-launched-to-make-blackpool-a-new-home-for-data-centre-development.aspx?date=22-05-2024), [Blackpool Dance Festival](https://blackpooldancefestival.com/festivals/blackpool-dance-festival/), [Showtown schedule](https://museumdevelopmentnorthwest.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/appxs1showtownfilmschedule.pdf).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Outside Lancashire County Council divisions`, because Blackpool is a unitary authority.

- `Campaign angle`
  Blackpool is Lancashire’s `spectacle + entertainment + digital-future` borough.

## Burnley

- `Current influence`
  `Sir James Anderson`, `Dave Fishwick`, `Cody Frost`, and `Tony Livesey` give Burnley a useful mix of sporting greatness, entrepreneurship, music edge and media reach.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Jimmy McIlroy` is the clearest single Burnley legend. Burnley’s wider football, mill and canal history does a lot of the brand work here, even before another individual face appears.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Panaz` is one of the strongest Burnley business stories because it combines design, textiles, sustainability and manufacturing. `Crow Wood` is a useful high-end hospitality anchor. Burnley also benefits from the wider East Lancashire making narrative and supply-chain story. Sources: [Panaz sustainability brochure](https://panaz.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/Panaz-Sustainability-Brochure-April-2023.pdf), [Lancashire large employers](https://www.lancashire.gov.uk/lancashire-insight/economy/major-employers/major-public-and-private-sector-employers/).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Burnley Central East`, `Burnley Central West`, `Burnley North East`, `Burnley South West`, and `Padiham and Burnley West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Burnley gives Lancashire `grit + enterprise + advanced textiles + premium leisure`.

## Chorley

- `Current influence`
  `Sir Lindsay Hoyle`, `Steve Pemberton`, `Kevin Simm`, and `Loui Batley` give Chorley a respectable mix of constitutional weight, acting, music and recognisable public personality.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Astley Hall`, the `Chorley Pals`, and `Chorley cake` culture are as important as any one historical celebrity because they make the borough feel storied, rooted and legible.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  Chorley’s official economic strategy says the borough expects strong labour-market growth and highlights opportunities in `advanced manufacturing`, `green`, and `technical` sectors. The borough also has a major town-centre regeneration programme and a monthly local-producer market economy. `Frederick’s Ice Cream` is a useful local brand anchor. Sources: [Chorley Economic Strategy](https://chorley.gov.uk/business-economy/economic-strategy-2022), [Town centre improvements](https://chorley.gov.uk/chorley-council/developments-regeneration/town-centre-improvements), [Food and craft market](https://chorley.gov.uk/foodandcraftmarket), [Chorley annual report](https://chorley.gov.uk/downloads/file/514/annual-report-2022-2023).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Chorley Central`, `Chorley Rural East`, `Chorley Rural West`, and `Chorley South`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Chorley gives Lancashire `Speaker-of-the-House stature + town-centre renewal + food-market warmth + growth-sector ambition`.

## Fylde

- `Current influence`
  Fylde is more `institution-led` than celebrity-led. `Councillor Karen Buckley` is a useful civic face, but in campaign terms the biggest public pull comes from `BAE Systems Warton`, `Royal Lytham & St Annes`, and `Lytham Festival` rather than a deep bench of local stars.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Les Dawson`, `George Formby`, and the borough’s long `aviation + golf + seafront elegance` identity provide the historic glamour layer.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `BAE Systems Warton` is one of the most important industrial anchors in Lancashire. Fylde’s official planning and tourism material also underlines the borough’s visitor-economy scale, while `Lytham Festival` and `Royal Lytham & St Annes` add major event prestige. Sources: [BAE Warton FalconWorks role](https://jobsearch.baesystems.com/job/quality-controller-00134269), [Fylde tourism figures](https://new.fylde.gov.uk/fylde-tourism-reaches-new-heights-with-record-3-31-million-visitors/), [Lytham Festival impact](https://new.fylde.gov.uk/lytham-festival-creates-9-million-boost-to-the-fylde-coast-economy/), [Fylde Local Plan spatial portrait](https://new.fylde.gov.uk/resident/planning/planning-policy-local-plan/fylde-local-plan-to-2042/spatial-portrait-of-fylde-key-issues-and-vision/).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Lytham`, `St Annes North`, `St Annes South`, `Fylde East`, and `Fylde West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Fylde gives Lancashire `future air systems + coastal affluence + golf + festival prestige`.

## Hyndburn

- `Current influence`
  `Jeanette Winterson` is the standout national cultural name. Beyond that, Hyndburn’s current influence is more `regeneration-led and civic` than celebrity-heavy.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  The `Accrington Pals`, `Accrington Nori`, and the `Haworth Art Gallery / Tiffany glass` story are the real legacy layer. Hyndburn feels strongest when marketed as a place of memory, industry and comeback rather than fame for fame’s sake.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  Hyndburn’s current public story is strongly regeneration-led: `Accrington Town Square`, `Great Harwood High Street Accelerator`, and new local business grants all show a borough trying to reposition itself for growth. The wider business base remains rooted in manufacturing and town-centre reinvention. Sources: [Accrington Town Square regeneration](https://www.hyndburnbc.gov.uk/news/construction-works-begin-as-accrington-town-square-regeneration-moves-forward/), [Hyndburn business grants](https://www.hyndburnbc.gov.uk/news/hyndburn-borough-council-launches-new-grants-scheme-to-support-local-businesses/), [Great Harwood High Street Accelerator](https://www.hyndburnbc.gov.uk/news/council-secures-additional-500k-towards-improvements-to-great-harwoods-high-streets/), [Leader and cabinet announcement](https://www.hyndburnbc.gov.uk/news/hyndburn-borough-council-leader-and-cabinet-announced/).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Accrington North`, `Accrington South`, `Accrington West & Oswaldtwistle Central`, and `Oswaldtwistle`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Hyndburn gives Lancashire `serious regeneration + literary credibility + East Lancashire town-centre comeback energy`.

## Lancaster

- `Current influence`
  `Wayne Hemingway` is a useful modern creative-industry name with Morecambe roots. `Cedric Robinson` is a powerful civic-cultural symbol for bay identity. The district’s real contemporary weight also comes from `Lancaster University` and the Eden / Morecambe regeneration story.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Eric Morecambe` is the dominant borough icon. `Thora Hird`, bay-guide heritage, and Morecambe’s old entertainment lineage deepen that story and make the district feel nationally memorable.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Lancaster University`, `Eden Project Morecambe`, `Heysham energy infrastructure`, the `Canal Quarter`, and a fast-growing visitor economy give the district a strong mix of knowledge, regeneration and tourism. Sources: [Economic Recovery Framework](https://www.lancaster.gov.uk/business/economic-recovery-framework), [Eden Project Morecambe funding](https://www.lancaster.gov.uk/news/2023/jan/eden-project-morecambe-awarded-50m-from-government-s-levelling-up-fund), [Canal Quarter masterplan](https://www.lancaster.gov.uk/news/2023/jul/canal-quarter-masterplan-formally-adopted), [Tourism economy 2026](https://www.lancaster.gov.uk/news/2026/jan/tourism-economy).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Lancaster Central`, `Heysham`, `Morecambe Central`, `Morecambe North`, `Morecambe South`, and `Lancaster Rural North`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Lancaster gives Lancashire `heritage + university power + Eden-scale optimism + bayfront renewal`.

## Pendle

- `Current influence`
  `Steven Burke` is the cleanest modern sporting name. On the economic and civic side, Pendle’s influence is carried at least as much by advanced manufacturers and regeneration leadership as by celebrity culture.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  The `Pendle Witches` are one of Lancashire’s biggest heritage assets full stop. `Wallace Hartley`, the Colne-born bandmaster associated with the Titanic story, adds a second powerful legend layer.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Silentnight`, `Rolls-Royce Barnoldswick`, `Hope Technology`, `Buoyant Upholstery`, and the borough’s partnership-led regeneration work through `PEARL` and the `Nelson Town Deal` make Pendle much more economically serious than an outsider might assume. Sources: [Pendle working in partnership](https://www.pendle.gov.uk/workinginpartnership), [Pendle Economic Growth Strategy 2025-2028](https://www.pendle.gov.uk/download/downloads/id/11583/economic_growth_strategy_2025_-_2028.pdf), [Buoyant funding story](https://www.pendle.gov.uk/news/article/277/one_of_pendles_biggest_employers_receives_funding_from_nelson_town_deal), [Pendle Rise regeneration](https://www.pendle.gov.uk/news/article/334/plans_to_revitalise_nelson_town_centre_moving_forward).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Pendle Central`, `Pendle Hill`, `Pendle Rural`, `Nelson East`, and `Brierfield & Nelson West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Pendle gives Lancashire `world-famous hill + elite cycling + advanced manufacturing + big-town regeneration`.

## Preston

- `Current influence`
  `Nick Park`, `Ranvir Singh`, and `Holly Bradshaw` are strong contemporary names because they connect Preston to animation, broadcasting and elite sport without losing warmth.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Sir Tom Finney` is the definitive Preston icon. `Edith Rigby` adds radical civic history and makes the city feel politically and culturally consequential, not just sporty.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Booths`, `James Hall & Co`, the `University of Lancashire`, and the wider education / retail / logistics mix make Preston one of the strongest all-round economic anchors in the county. The city also feeds the county’s space, skills and creator narratives. Sources: [Lancashire large employers](https://www.lancashire.gov.uk/lancashire-insight/economy/major-employers/major-public-and-private-sector-employers/), [James Hall automation](https://www.jameshall.co.uk/james-hall-co-ltd-streamlines-wholesale-and-retail-planning-with-relex/), [Lancashire in Space Partnership](https://www.lancashire.ac.uk/news/one-giant-leap-as-lancashire-launches-new-space-partnership).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Preston Central East`, `Preston City`, `Preston East`, `Preston Rural`, and `Preston South West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Preston gives Lancashire `animation + football heritage + retail/logistics strength + university-led future skills`.

## Ribble Valley

- `Current influence`
  `Nigel Haworth` and `Stephen Atkinson` are useful current names, but Ribble Valley is one of the places where `hospitality brands, producers and landscape prestige` often matter more than celebrity.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Clitheroe Castle`, the `Stonyhurst / Tolkien association`, and Bowland-facing rural heritage do a lot of the symbolic work. This borough sells `quality and memory` more than headline fame.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  Ribble Valley’s influence comes less from heavy industry and more from `high-value food`, `premium hospitality`, and `rural quality`. `Northcote`, `Mrs Kirkham’s`, Bowland-facing tourism, and upscale food culture are the strongest commercial signals. Sources: [Northcote](https://www.northcote.com/), [Ribble Valley food and tourism context](https://www.visitlancashire.com/dbimgs/RibbleBookletWeb.pdf), [Ribble Valley mayor story](https://www.ribblevalley.gov.uk/news/article/243/-farmers-friend-louise-is-the-new-ribble-valley-mayor), [Ribble Valley council leadership context](https://www.ribblevalley.gov.uk/news/article/326/new-chief-executive-at-ribble-valley-borough-council).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Clitheroe`, `Ribble Valley North East`, and `Ribble Valley South West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Ribble Valley gives Lancashire `quality + landscape + food prestige + affluent rural confidence`.

## Rossendale

- `Current influence`
  `Jane Horrocks` is the clearest cultural name. In present-day place-marketing terms, Rossendale’s influence is also being built through digital-skills and regeneration leadership rather than pure celebrity.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  The `Bacup Britannia Coconut Dancers`, valley music-and-performance culture, and the Whitaker orbit give Rossendale a properly distinctive legacy layer.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  Rossendale’s strongest current official story is `digital and regeneration`: the `Digital Tech Hub`, the IN4-backed upskilling push, `Pride in Place`, and broader borough regeneration. Sources: [Rossendale Digital Tech Hub](https://www.rossendale.gov.uk/news/article/10691/rossendale-council-launches-digital-tech-hub-and-upskilling-programme-to-boost-local-economy), [Pride in Place](https://www.rossendale.gov.uk/news/article/10770/rossendale-s-pride-in-place-regeneration-plan-given-green-light), [Rossendale Works](https://www.rossendale.gov.uk/news/article/10789/council-confirms-continued-investment-in-successful-rossendale-works), [Rossendale leadership](https://www.rossendale.gov.uk/councillors-1/leader-council-portfolio-holders).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Mid Rossendale`, `Rossendale East`, `Rossendale South`, and `Rossendale West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Rossendale gives Lancashire `creative valley identity + digital upskilling + town renewal + walking and landscape credibility`.

## South Ribble

- `Current influence`
  South Ribble is another place where `industrial and innovation leadership` matters more than celebrity. `Tom Lord` is useful in the stakeholder layer, but the borough’s public weight comes mainly from the Samlesbury / Leyland corridor.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Leyland Motors`, truck-making heritage, and `Samlesbury Hall` give South Ribble a stronger memory-bank than people often realise.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  South Ribble has one of the strongest business stories in Lancashire because it carries the `Samlesbury Innovation Hub`, `Samlesbury Enterprise Zone`, `AMRC North West`, `Leyland Trucks`, and the `Budweiser / Protium hydrogen` story, alongside the wider `Lancashire Central` employment corridor. Sources: [Samlesbury Innovation Hub](https://news.lancashire.gov.uk/news/design-team-appointed-for-gbp-13-2m-samlesbury-innovation-hub-as-lancashire-moves-project-into-delivery), [Samlesbury Enterprise Zone](https://samlesburyez.com/supply-chain/), [AMRC North West](https://www.amrc.co.uk/pages/amrc-north-west), [Samlesbury Net Zero](https://www.marketinglancashire.com/news/protium-and-budweiser-brewing-group-begin-plans-for-hydrogen-energy-project-at-samlesbury-brewery/), [Lancashire Central](https://investinlancashire.com/sites-and-premises/lancashire-central/), [Lancashire large employers](https://www.lancashire.gov.uk/lancashire-insight/economy/major-employers/major-public-and-private-sector-employers/), [South Ribble leadership](https://southribble.gov.uk/news/article/15/new-leader-appointed-at-south-ribble-borough-council).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `South Ribble East`, `South Ribble West`, and `Penwortham West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  South Ribble gives Lancashire `innovation corridor + aerospace supply chain + hydrogen + national-scale job creation`.

## West Lancashire

- `Current influence`
  `Jessica Gagen` is the clearest current crossover name because she brings glamour, STEM credibility and county-ambassador potential. West Lancashire’s wider influence, though, is mainly built through food production, logistics and regeneration rather than a huge celebrity bench.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  The borough’s deeper identity is carried less by singular famous people and more by `market-garden productivity`, `Ormskirk market-town heritage`, and `Skelmersdale’s new-town story`.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  West Lancashire is strong in `agri-food`, `business support`, and `Skelmersdale regeneration`. `Flavourfresh`, `Fiddler’s Lancashire Crisps`, `Walkers` at West Pimbo, and the council-backed `Collaborative Innovation Programme` are all useful parts of the story. Sources: [West Lancashire business support](https://www.westlancs.gov.uk/business/business-advice-and-support/support-to-grow-your-business.aspx), [Collaborative Innovation Programme](https://www.westlancs.gov.uk/news/collaborative-innovation-programme-launched-to-west-lancashire-smes.aspx), [Skelmersdale Masterplan](https://www.westlancs.gov.uk/news/skelmersdale-masterplan-approved-by-west-lancashire-borough-council.aspx), [West Lancashire regeneration](https://www.westlancs.gov.uk/business/regeneration-in-west-lancashire.aspx), [Fiddler's Lancashire Crisps](https://www.fiddlerslancashirecrisps.co.uk/).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Burscough & Rufford`, `West Lancashire East`, `West Lancashire North`, and `West Lancashire West`.

- `Campaign angle`
  West Lancashire gives Lancashire `food production + town reinvention + growth support + new ambassador talent`.

## Wyre

- `Current influence`
  Wyre’s clearest public figures are often `business families and enterprise leaders` rather than celebrity names. `Alfie Boe` is a useful premium cultural name because Fleetwood gives the borough a recognisable musical voice. The `Dewlay` team and the `Fisherman's Friend` business story are especially useful because they feel unmistakably local.

- `Past legends / legacy icons`
  `Sir Peter Hesketh-Fleetwood`, the fishing-port story, and Fleetwood’s harbour heritage give Wyre a strong founding myth and a real industrial-memory layer.

- `Businesses and innovation`
  `Hillhouse Technology Enterprise Zone` is the borough’s biggest future-facing asset, especially around advanced manufacturing and clean energy. `Dewlay` adds food production and renewable-energy credibility. `Invest in Wyre` and the borough’s business-grant schemes reinforce a growth story. Sources: [Hillhouse Technology Enterprise Zone](https://www.wyre.gov.uk/economic-development-1/hillhouse-international-enterprise-zone), [Wyre business grant scheme](https://www.wyre.gov.uk/news/article/548/new-business-grant-scheme-to-launch-this-autumn-supporting-growth-innovation-and-sustainability-in-wyre), [Invest in Wyre](https://www.wyre.gov.uk/news/article/535/wyre-council-launches-new-invest-in-wyre-website-to-support-business-growth-and-investment), [Dewlay](https://www.discoverwyre.co.uk/attractions-1/dewlay-cheesemakers), [Wyre leadership](https://www.wyre.gov.uk/news/article/321/wyre-council-confirms-new-chief-executive).

- `Division / ward anchors`
  `Fleetwood East`, `Fleetwood West & Cleveleys West`, `Poulton le Fylde`, `Thornton & Hambleton`, and `Wyre Rural Central`.

- `Campaign angle`
  Wyre gives Lancashire `clean-energy enterprise-zone credibility + iconic products + coastal industry`.

## How to use this

- Use this as an `outreach and filming priority map`, not a final public ranking.
- Boroughs with the strongest combined `people + business + future-project` story right now are:
  `South Ribble`, `Preston`, `Blackburn with Darwen`, `Lancaster`, `Fylde`, `Burnley`, and `Pendle`.
- Boroughs with the strongest `regeneration comeback` story are:
  `Hyndburn`, `Rossendale`, `West Lancashire`, `Pendle`, and `Blackpool`.
- Boroughs with the strongest `premium / lifestyle / quality` tone are:
  `Ribble Valley`, `Fylde`, and parts of `Lancaster`.
- Boroughs with the strongest `legend + memory + symbolic depth` layer are:
  `Blackpool`, `Lancaster`, `Pendle`, `Preston`, `Burnley`, and `Blackburn with Darwen`.
